Head of Operations

Last updated: April 2026

Founder & CEO

Remote (U.S. only)

$180K base + performance bonus

Mission

Own the operating system at Formulation Factory - weekly cadence, team accountability, supply chain, HR, finance ops, and process infrastructure - so the business scales from $13M to $20M+ without the Founder being the bottleneck on day-to-day execution.

Role Context

The company has functional leads across growth and marketing, CX, and creative. What's missing is someone who owns the operating system that ties all of it together - the weekly cadence, KPI accountability, HR, supply chain, finance ops, process documentation, technology stack, and cross-functional coordination that keeps a fast-growing brand from breaking.

This is a senior leadership position for someone who has been the operational backbone of a DTC brand that scaled from $10M to $30M+. You've implemented EOS or a similar operating system, you live in Notion, and you use AI as a daily tool - not a novelty. You build the machine that makes everyone else faster.

You are biased toward speed and decisive action, but you protect the team from chaos with clear priorities and simple systems.

You're a 3+ monitor person. You have Notion, Slack, your P&L, and a supply chain dashboard open simultaneously - not because someone told you to, but because that's how your brain works. You're wired to monitor, catch, and fix before anyone else notices.

Scorecard - Outcomes

This is how we measure success. Not effort, not hours - outcomes with metrics and deadlines.

By Day 30
  • Fully embedded in Notion: reviewed entire workspace, understands current structure, and has begun organizing and consolidating. Has access to all critical dashboards, docs, and databases.
  • Team audit complete: reviewed every seat on the team, had 1:1s with all direct reports and key contributors, and has a written assessment of who is in the right seat vs who is not.
  • 1:1 structure established: a proper weekly 1:1 cadence is installed with every manager. Each manager understands the format, expectations, and documentation standard. You are reviewing 1:1 notes weekly.
  • Weekly operating cadence launched: a single weekly leadership meeting (L10-style or equivalent) is in place - scorecard, issues list, clear owner per metric - and is followed by all functional leads.
  • Risk map delivered: top 5 operational risks to the business identified (supply chain concentration, compliance exposure, key-person dependencies, etc.) with a mitigation plan for each. Presented to CEO.
By Day 60
  • Finance ops transferred: all bills, subscriptions, and vendor payments are routed to a centralized finance@ workflow and managed by you or your designee. No bills floating in the CEO's inbox.
  • Daily P&L review: you are reviewing P&L performance across SKUs and channels daily, flagging anomalies, and bringing a weekly finance summary to the operating meeting. Working with the bookkeeper, not replacing them.
  • Vendor and contract audit complete: every vendor contract, 3PL agreement, and CM relationship reviewed. Where are terms bad? Where are we overpaying? Where is a vendor a single point of failure? Written report with renegotiation or replacement recommendations delivered to CEO.
  • Google Drive + Notion audit complete: file structure is rationalized, permissions are cleaned up, and the team has a single source of truth for every function.
  • HR processes live: internal hiring funnel, onboarding checklist, and offboarding process are documented and running. Payroll is being overseen (provider-managed) without CEO involvement.
  • Technology stack audit delivered: every SaaS tool across every department is catalogued with cost, owner, and recommendation (keep, replace, consolidate). Presented to CEO for approval.
By Day 90
  • Hiring underway: open seats identified based on growth forecasting and team gaps. Every hire uses the scorecard methodology (mission, outcomes, competencies) with structured interviews. All new hires are on-track against their 30-day milestones within 45 days of start.
  • Fully running the organization day-to-day: the Founder spends <5 hrs/week on recurring ops. You own the operating rhythm, the team cadence, and the cross-functional coordination.
  • Business forecasting: a rolling 13-week cash forecast is live and updated weekly. You are forecasting business growth and directional planning alongside the CEO - revenue targets, headcount plan, capacity planning.
  • Team performance managed: every team member has a written scorecard (3-5 KPIs). B- and C-players are identified with clear improvement plans or exit timelines.
By Month 6
  • Supply chain dialed: hero SKU in-stock rate ≥ 98% across Amazon and DTC. Every SKU has a documented reorder point, lead time, and safety stock level. No stockouts on top 5 SKUs for 3+ consecutive months.
  • 3+ workflows automated: reporting, CX macros, purchase orders, content briefs, or similar - automated or AI-assisted, reducing manual hours by ≥30% without adding headcount.
  • Internal AI solutions deployed: at least 2 internal AI tools or workflows (custom GPTs, automation, reporting bots) that the team uses daily.
  • No single point of failure: each core function (growth and marketing, DTC, CX, supply chain) has a clear owner, documented SOPs, and at least 1 trained backup.
  • New hires producing: all new hires are onboarded, have scorecards, and are hitting their milestones in the weekly rhythm.
  • Operating system is self-sustaining: weekly cadence, scorecards, 1:1s, hiring processes, and reporting run without you having to enforce them manually every week. The system scales to $20M+ without adding layers of management.
  • Founder spends <2 hrs/week on ops. Their calendar is majority acquisitions, partnerships, content, and long-term brand strategy.
  • Cash forecast tracking within ±10% variance for 3+ consecutive months. DTC SaaS stack rationalized. The team is measurably stronger than when you arrived.
Ongoing KPIs

Once the system is built, these are the numbers you own and review weekly. This is your scoreboard.

  • Hero SKU in-stock rate: target ≥ 98% across Amazon and DTC.
  • Cash forecast variance: rolling 13-week forecast within ±10% of actuals.
  • Team scorecard completion: 100% of team members have active scorecards with 3-5 KPIs, reviewed weekly in 1:1s.
  • SOP coverage: % of core workflows with documented, current SOPs. Target 100% of critical processes.
  • Vendor cost savings: tracked quarterly vs. baseline at hire. Target measurable savings through renegotiation and consolidation.
  • Founder ops hours: weekly hours CEO spends on recurring ops. Target <2 hrs/week by month 6.
  • Hiring pipeline velocity: time from open seat to offer accepted. Target <30 days for non-executive roles.
  • Workflow automation savings: manual hours reduced through AI/automation across automated workflows. Target ≥ 30% reduction.

Explicit Boundaries

This role does not own:

  • Growth and marketing strategy, paid media, and channel strategy - that belongs to the growth and marketing function
  • Product vision, formulation philosophy, or brand worldview - those belong to the Founder
  • Creative direction - you ensure creative output is resourced and on schedule

You own the operating infrastructure that all of those functions run on. When Growth wants to scale spend 3x, you're the one who ensures fulfillment, CX, and inventory can handle it without breaking.

Decision Rights

Owns: Operating cadence, supply chain, inventory, vendor/3PL relationships, HR (hiring, onboarding, offboarding, payroll oversight), process/SOP documentation, technology stack, team scorecards, financial reporting/forecasting, Notion and Google Drive organization, and cross-functional coordination.

Reports to: Founder & CEO.

Collaborates with: Growth and marketing team on DTC, Amazon, and paid media, eCommerce on storefront and subscription ops, CX on support workflows, and all agency partners.

This Role Is Not
  • A COO title for someone who wants to be chief of everything - this is ops, not the whole company
  • A generalist who needs to be educated on supplement supply chains or DTC fulfillment
  • A project manager who tracks tasks but doesn't own outcomes
  • A cautious executive who slows things down to build consensus
  • A reactive tornado - all gas, no steering wheel
Ideal Candidate
  • 3-7 years in operations at a DTC brand that scaled from $10M to $30M+ - you've lived through the scaling pain and know what breaks
  • Supplement, health, wellness, or fitness industry experience strongly preferred
  • Expert in EOS or a similar operating system - you've implemented it, not just read the book
  • Notion power user - you've built and managed an entire company workspace, not just a few pages
  • Direct experience managing supply chain, contract manufacturers, and 3PLs
  • Managed all HR functions at a small company: hiring pipelines, onboarding, offboarding, payroll oversight
  • Strong financial literacy - daily P&L review, cash forecasting, vendor management, and bill pay oversight
  • Proficient with AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) as daily operational tools, not experiments
  • U.S.-based - this is a U.S.-only hire

Bonus: You've built custom GPTs, automation workflows, or internal AI tools that your team actually uses daily.
