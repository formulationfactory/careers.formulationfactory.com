Operations Remote (U.S.) Full-Time

Head of Operations

Founder & CEO

Remote (U.S. preferred, must overlap AST)

$120K–$180K base + performance bonuses

Mission

Own the operating rhythm, supply chain, process infrastructure, and team accountability at Formulation Factory — so the business can scale from $13M to $20M+ without the Founder being the bottleneck on day-to-day execution.

Role Context

We already have a Head of Growth, Head of Amazon, and functional leads across CX and creative. What we don't have is someone who owns the operating system that ties all of it together — the weekly cadence, the KPI accountability, the supply chain, the process documentation, and the cross-functional coordination that keeps a fast-growing brand from breaking.

This is not a C-suite role. It's a high-output senior leadership position for someone who builds the systems that let a small team operate like a much bigger one. You are biased toward speed and decisive action, but you protect the team from chaos with clear priorities and simple systems.

If you've run ops at a DTC or supplement brand doing $5M–$30M, know what in-stock rate means for Amazon revenue, and have strong opinions about how to run a weekly operating meeting — keep reading.

Scorecard — Outcomes

This is how we measure success. Not effort, not hours — outcomes with metrics and deadlines.

By Month 3
  • The Founder spends <5 hrs/week on recurring ops meetings (supply chain, CX, inventory, vendor calls)
  • A single weekly operating cadence is in place — scorecard, issues list, clear owner per metric — and is followed 4 out of 4 weeks by all functional leads
  • Full inventory and supply chain audit completed. Every SKU has a documented reorder point, lead time, and safety stock level
By Month 6
  • Supply chain: Hero SKU in-stock rate ≥ 98% across Amazon and DTC. No stockouts on top 5 SKUs for 3+ consecutive months
  • Process: SOPs documented for all core workflows — purchase orders, inventory replenishment, CX escalations, new product launch checklist, vendor onboarding
  • Financial ops: A rolling 13-week cash forecast is updated weekly and tracking within ±10% variance
  • Reporting: Weekly operating dashboard live with inventory health, fulfillment metrics, CX response times, and margin by channel
By Month 9
  • At least 3 major workflows (e.g., reporting, CX macros, purchase order process, content briefs) are automated or AI-assisted, reducing manual hours by ≥30% without adding headcount
  • Company topline run rate is up 30%+ vs prior year with contribution margin equal or higher than at hire date
  • Vendor and 3PL relationships are managed without Founder involvement on day-to-day issues
By Month 12
  • No single point of failure: each core function (Amazon ops, DTC ops, CX, Supply Chain) has a clear owner, documented SOPs, and at least 1 trained backup
  • Every team member has a written scorecard (3–5 KPIs each) and a weekly 1:1 rhythm with their manager
  • 100% of B- and C-players identified: either improved to hitting their scorecard for 3 consecutive months, or exited and replaced
  • The Founder spends <2 hrs/week on ops. Their calendar is majority acquisitions, partnerships, content, and long-term brand strategy
  • The operating system you've built can support $20M+ without adding layers of management

Explicit Boundaries

This role does not own:

  • Channel strategy for Amazon, DTC, or paid media — those belong to the Head of Growth and Head of Amazon
  • Product vision, formulation philosophy, or brand worldview — those belong to the Founder
  • Creative direction — you ensure creative output is resourced and on schedule, not that you set the vision

You do own the operating infrastructure that all of those functions run on. When Growth wants to scale spend 3x, you're the one who makes sure fulfillment, CX, and inventory can handle it without breaking.

Decision Rights

Owns: Operating cadence, supply chain and inventory management, vendor and 3PL relationships, process documentation, team scorecards, financial reporting and forecasting, and cross-functional coordination.

Reports to: Founder & CEO.

Collaborates with: Head of Growth on DTC and paid media scaling, Head of Amazon on marketplace ops, Director of eCommerce on fulfillment and subscription ops, CX Lead on support workflows, and all agency partners.

This Role Is Not
  • A COO title for someone who wants to be chief of everything — this is ops, not the whole company
  • A generalist who needs to be educated on supplement supply chains or DTC fulfillment
  • A project manager who tracks tasks but doesn't own outcomes
  • A cautious executive who slows things down to build consensus
Ideal Candidate
  • 3–7 years in operations at a DTC, Amazon, or CPG brand doing $5M–$30M — you've lived through the scaling pain
  • Supplement, health, wellness, or fitness industry experience strongly preferred
  • Direct experience managing supply chain, 3PLs, and contract manufacturers
  • Strong financial literacy — you can own a forecast and connect operational decisions to margin impact
  • You've built operating cadences and team accountability systems that actually stuck
  • Comfortable using AI (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) daily to compress manual work

Bonus: You've built custom GPTs, automation workflows, or AI-powered systems that meaningfully changed how a team operates.
