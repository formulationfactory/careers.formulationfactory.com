Executive Remote (U.S.) Full-Time

Chief Operating Officer

Formulation Factory is looking for its co-pilot. Not a traditional executive who manages from altitude — a genuine operator who thinks like a founder, moves like an athlete, and is as obsessed with performance as the person who started this company.

Founder & CEO

Remote (U.S. preferred, must overlap AST)

$150K–$250K base + equity & performance incentives

Role Purpose

This role exists because the business has outgrown what one person can run. We are at $9M ARR and scaling hard. The COO takes ownership of day-to-day execution across every function — growth, operations, Amazon, DTC, finance, supply chain, and team — so the Founder can focus on product truth, brand philosophy, and long-term vision. You are not here to sit above the work. You are here to be in it, alongside the CEO, at the same speed and intensity.

If you have multiple monitors running simultaneously, know what your cortisol looks like at noon, have a strong opinion about magnesium glycinate vs threonate, and have built or scaled something real in the DTC supplement space — keep reading.

Core Accountabilities

1. Full-Stack Operations Ownership

Own day-to-day execution across all business functions. Set priorities, sequence initiatives, and drive delivery to an elite standard. The business runs on your cadence. You design the operating rhythm — weekly reviews, KPI dashboards, cross-functional syncs, and accountability mechanisms — and you enforce it. Nothing drifts on your watch.

2. Channel and Revenue Execution

Deep, hands-on familiarity with every revenue channel: Amazon FBA (listings, PPC, account health, A+ content, brand store), Shopify DTC (CRO, Rebuy, Skio subscriptions, Klaviyo flows, landing pages), TikTok Shop and organic, and paid media across Meta and Google. You don't just manage people who do these things — you understand them well enough to diagnose, fix, and improve them yourself when needed.

3. AI-First Operating Model

You use AI the way serious operators use leverage. Claude, ChatGPT, Perplexity, custom GPTs, automation workflows — these are daily tools, not novelties. You build systems that make the team faster. You identify where AI can replace manual work, compress decision cycles, and create competitive advantages our competitors haven't caught up to yet.

4. Team Leadership and Development

Directly lead a high-output remote team across Growth, Operations, and CX. Set uncompromising standards, give direct feedback, hold people accountable to results, and develop talent relentlessly. You recruit people who are better than average at things you are not and you never settle for B players when A players exist.

5. Financial and Commercial Acumen

Own the operating P&L alongside the Founder. Understand contribution margin, CAC, LTV, TACOS, ACOS, MER, and the unit economics of both Amazon and DTC channels at a detailed level. You can build a forecast, read a QuickBooks report, and connect financial performance to operational decisions without needing a CFO to translate.

6. Product and Supply Chain Intelligence

Understand supplement formats — capsules, powders, gummies, softgels — at a level that lets you have intelligent conversations with contract manufacturers, evaluate formulation tradeoffs, and manage supply chain timelines without being misled. You don't need to be a formulator, but you need to know enough to hold vendors accountable and protect product quality and margin.

7. Cross-Functional Integration

Act as the central integrator across growth, ops, supply chain, finance, and people. Resolve trade-offs decisively, eliminate silos, and prevent escalation bottlenecks from reaching the Founder. You protect the CEO's time from execution decisions so it can be spent on the things only the CEO can do.

Explicit Boundaries

This role does not own:

  • Product vision, core formulation philosophy, or brand worldview — those belong to the Founder
  • Final authority on brand standards or long-term mission — you execute within them and challenge constructively when needed
  • Creative direction for the brand — you ensure creative output is resourced and on schedule, not that you set the creative vision

You are expected to push back hard when you disagree, bring data when you challenge direction, and never simply comply when you see a better path. But the brand's non-negotiable standards — ingredient transparency, third-party testing, no misleading marketing claims — are the foundation you operate on.

Decision Rights & Key Interfaces

Owns: All execution-level decisions, resource allocation, operational priorities, team performance management, and channel-level strategy within guardrails set by the Founder.

Reports to: Founder & CEO.

Collaborates closely with: Head of Growth on paid media and DTC performance, Brand Manager on Amazon and content execution, Head of Operations on supply chain and 3PL, and all agency partners.

This Role Is Not
  • A traditional COO who manages from a dashboard and delegates everything downward
  • A generalist operator who needs to be educated on the supplement or DTC ecommerce space
  • A cautious executive who slows things down to build consensus
  • Someone who uses AI occasionally when it's convenient

This is a co-founder-level operator who is genuinely obsessed with building something great, treats speed as a competitive advantage, and brings the same energy to a Monday morning dashboard review as they do to a major product launch.

Year-One Success Profile
  • The Founder's time is freed from day-to-day execution decisions and the business runs at the same speed or faster
  • A clear operating cadence is established and the team is held to it consistently
  • Amazon and DTC channels are running at measurably higher efficiency with cleaner reporting and faster decision cycles
  • AI and automation tools are embedded across at least three major workflows that were previously manual
  • The team is stronger — higher standards, better accountability, fewer underperformers
  • Revenue is growing and contribution margin is holding or improving
Ideal Candidate
  • 5+ years operating in DTC ecommerce with direct hands-on experience on Amazon FBA, Shopify, and paid social — not just overseeing teams that do these things
  • Supplement, health, wellness, biohacking, or fitness industry experience strongly preferred — you understand the products, the customer, the regulatory environment, and the category dynamics
  • A genuine, personal obsession with health optimization — you track your bloodwork, you know your stack, you follow the science
  • Proven AI power user — you have built workflows, custom GPTs, or automation systems that have meaningfully impacted how a team operates
  • Strong financial literacy — you can own a P&L, build a forecast, and connect operational decisions to financial outcomes without translation
  • High-volume operator by nature — you manage complexity across multiple workstreams simultaneously and you thrive in that environment
  • You have built or scaled a brand, a team, or a business unit from something small to something real
