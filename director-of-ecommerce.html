Director of eCommerce

Last updated: April 2026

Founder & CEO

Remote (U.S. only)

$180K base + performance bonus

Mission

Own all revenue-generating channels at Formulation Factory - Amazon, Shopify DTC, TikTok Shop, paid media, and creative strategy - and turn a $13M brand with a dominant Amazon channel into a diversified, multi-channel revenue engine scaling toward $20M+.

Role Context

Formulation Factory does $13M+ ARR. Amazon is already a machine - dialed in with a Brand Manager running day-to-day and a PPC agency managing ads. TikTok Shop is completely not set up. Shopify DTC is live but unoptimized for conversion - Klaviyo is minimally configured, landing pages need optimization, and there's no systematic funnel infrastructure connecting paid media to purchase.

This role owns the full revenue picture. You oversee Amazon (which is running), build out DTC and TikTok Shop (which are not), own paid media strategy and creative direction, and ensure every dollar spent on acquisition has a matching, optimized conversion experience on the other end.

You're technical enough to build landing pages in Shopify yourself and strategic enough to decide which ones to build. You're comfortable managing agencies and direct reports. You think in funnels, offers, and unit economics - not just traffic and impressions.

This role graduates into a CRO-level position as the business scales. If you want to own the revenue engine at a brand that's scaling fast with real products and real customers, this is it.

Scorecard - Outcomes

This is how we measure success. Not effort, not hours - outcomes with metrics and deadlines.

By Day 30
  • Full channel audit complete: Amazon health check (PPC performance, listing quality, account health), Shopify storefront audit (conversion rate, page speed, mobile experience, checkout flow), TikTok Shop assessment (what exists, what's needed to launch), and paid media audit (active accounts, spend, ROAS, creative performance).
  • Klaviyo audit delivered: every existing flow and campaign reviewed. Gap analysis showing what's missing vs. what a best-in-class Klaviyo setup looks like. Priority build list with revenue projections.
  • Agency and vendor assessment: reviewed all agency relationships (PPC, creative, media buying). Recommendation to CEO: keep, replace, expand, or bring in-house.
  • Top 10 revenue opportunities identified: a prioritized list of the highest-ROI moves across all channels - quick wins and structural builds. Presented to CEO with estimated impact and timeline.
  • Creative asset inventory: catalogued all existing ad creative, UGC, landing pages, and content assets. Identified gaps in the creative pipeline.
By Day 60
  • Shopify CRO quick wins shipped: hero PDPs optimized, cart experience improved, mobile experience tightened, trust elements added. Measurable conversion rate improvement vs. baseline.
  • Core Klaviyo flows live: welcome series, abandoned cart, browse abandonment, post-purchase education, and win-back flows are built, tested, and generating revenue. Not placeholders - real flows with segmentation and proper copy.
  • Existing landing pages refined: current landing pages audited, optimized, and A/B tested. New landing pages built where gaps exist. Each paired to a specific offer and ad angle. Conversion tracked and reported.
  • First bundle offers created: at least 2 bundle/offer structures tested on DTC (e.g., buy 2 get 1, starter kits, subscribe-and-save incentives). AOV impact measured.
  • Weekly revenue dashboard live: all-channel dashboard covering Amazon revenue, DTC revenue, TikTok Shop, paid media spend, MER/ROAS, Klaviyo attributed revenue, conversion rate by funnel, and AOV. Updated weekly, reviewed in operating meeting.
  • Paid media strategy defined: channel allocation (Meta, TikTok, Google), budget framework, creative testing methodology, and scaling criteria documented. Whether managed by agency or in-house, you own the strategy.
By Day 90
  • DTC revenue measurably up from baseline: combination of CRO improvements, Klaviyo revenue, new landing pages, and paid media optimization driving real revenue growth.
  • TikTok Shop live: products listed, initial content published, affiliate/creator infrastructure in progress. GMV tracking from day one.
  • Creative pipeline running: a repeatable system for producing ad creative - hooks, angles, formats documented. New ads shipping weekly, not monthly. Working with creative team and/or UGC creators.
  • A/B testing rhythm established: at least 2-3 tests running at any time on Shopify (headlines, layouts, CTAs, pricing presentation, trust elements). Results logged and informing next tests.
  • Subscription experience optimized: subscribe-and-save offer improved on PDPs, post-purchase subscription upsell in place, churn reduction tactics deployed. Tracking subscriber growth, churn rate, and LTV by cohort.
  • Full funnel documented: every active offer mapped end-to-end: ad > landing page > purchase > post-purchase > retention. Playbook documented so anyone on the team can see how money flows.
By Month 6
  • DTC is a real channel: DTC revenue is 15%+ of total (up from ~5% at hire) with profitable unit economics and MER within range of Amazon's contribution margin.
  • TikTok Shop generating meaningful GMV: products selling, affiliate/creator program producing, and a clear optimization roadmap in place.
  • 3+ tested, ready-to-scale funnels per paid channel: offer + landing page + ad angles with playbooks documented. At any point the team can increase spend 2-3x without conversion rate dropping or ops breaking.
  • Klaviyo driving 15-20%+ of DTC revenue: through flows and campaigns combined. List growing. Segmentation in place. Revenue per recipient improving.
  • Amazon maintained or growing: under your strategic oversight, Amazon Brand Manager and PPC agency are performing. You're reviewing weekly, catching issues, and pushing improvements - not running it day-to-day.
  • Creative is a machine: new ads, angles, and landing pages shipping weekly. Testing velocity is high. Winners are identified fast and scaled. Losers are killed fast.
  • All-channel revenue growing 30%+ vs. prior period: with contribution margin maintained or improved. The revenue engine you've built can absorb more spend without breaking.
Ongoing KPIs

Once the system is built, these are the numbers you own and review weekly. This is your scoreboard.

  • Total revenue (all channels): Amazon + DTC + TikTok Shop. Tracked weekly, reported in operating meeting.
  • DTC revenue as % of total: target ≥ 15% by month 6, growing quarter over quarter.
  • MER / blended ROAS: total revenue divided by total ad spend across all paid channels. Target improving or stable as spend scales.
  • Shopify conversion rate: site-wide and by landing page/funnel. Target improving vs. baseline through ongoing CRO.
  • Klaviyo attributed revenue: as % of DTC revenue. Target 15-20%+ through flows and campaigns combined.
  • AOV: average order value on DTC. Tracked weekly, influenced through bundles, upsells, and subscription offers.
  • Subscriber count + churn rate: net subscriber growth and monthly churn. LTV by cohort tracked quarterly.
  • TikTok Shop GMV: monthly gross merchandise value. Tracked from launch with growth targets set after 90-day baseline.
  • Creative velocity: number of new ad creatives shipped per week. Target consistent weekly output with clear test/kill/scale cadence.
  • Ready-to-scale funnels: number of tested, profitable funnels per paid channel. Target ≥ 3 per channel at all times.

Explicit Boundaries

This role does not own:

  • Product vision, formulation philosophy, or brand worldview - those belong to the Founder
  • Operations, supply chain, HR, or finance ops - that belongs to the operations function
  • Brand voice and positioning - you execute within brand guidelines set by the Founder

You own everything that generates revenue. From the ad to the landing page to the checkout to the repeat purchase across every channel. Operations makes sure it ships. You make sure it sells.

Decision Rights

Owns: All revenue channel strategy and execution (Amazon oversight, Shopify DTC, TikTok Shop), paid media strategy, creative strategy, Klaviyo email/SMS, CRO, landing pages, subscription experience, bundle/offer strategy, agency management, and all-channel revenue reporting.

Reports to: Founder & CEO.

Collaborates with: Operations on fulfillment and inventory, creative team on ad and content production, CX on post-purchase experience, and all agency partners.

This Role Is Not
  • A Shopify developer who waits for tickets - you own the strategy and the execution
  • A media buyer who only knows how to run ads but can't build what happens after the click
  • A generalist marketer with ecommerce as one of five responsibilities
  • An email manager who sends newsletters - you build a revenue engine
  • Someone who manages Amazon and ignores DTC, or vice versa - you own the full picture
Ideal Candidate
  • 4-7 years owning revenue at a DTC brand doing $5M-$30M+ - you've scaled channels, not just managed them
  • Deep Shopify expertise - Liquid, theme customization, app ecosystem (Klaviyo, Skio/ReCharge, Rebuy, Okendo, or equivalents)
  • Built and managed Klaviyo flows that drove measurable revenue, not just opens and clicks
  • Proven CRO chops - you've run tests, improved conversion rates, and can talk through your methodology
  • Paid media strategy experience - you've owned or closely managed Meta, TikTok, and/or Google campaigns with real budget
  • Amazon fluency - you understand the platform well enough to oversee a Brand Manager and PPC agency and catch when things are off
  • TikTok Shop experience preferred - you've launched or scaled a TikTok Shop, or have deep platform knowledge
  • Supplement, health, wellness, or fitness brand experience strongly preferred
  • Can build and ship landing pages fast - you don't need a developer for every change
  • Proficient with AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) as daily workflow tools
  • U.S.-based - this is a U.S.-only hire

Bonus: You've built a brand's DTC channel from near-zero to a meaningful revenue contributor. You've been the person who turned "we're mostly Amazon" into "we're a real multi-channel brand."
