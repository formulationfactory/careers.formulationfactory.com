Head of Social Media

Last updated: April 2026

Director of eCommerce

Remote (U.S. only)

$80K base + performance bonus

Mission

Own Formulation Factory's organic social presence across every platform - creating content, directing creative, managing high-volume publishing, and building an audience that trusts us - so organic social becomes a real revenue and brand-building lever, not an afterthought.

Role Context

This is not a "post and monitor mentions" role. You are a content creator and creative strategist who also happens to be a volume publishing operator. You ideate content, script it, create it yourself, direct others to create it, and then ensure it ships across every platform at a pace that keeps us top-of-mind in a category where attention is everything.

You'll work hand-in-hand with the Creative Strategist and creative team to produce organic, value-add content - educational, authority-building, and community-driven. But you're not waiting for briefs to land in your inbox. You're bringing ideas, creating assets, using AI tools to move faster, and pushing the pipeline forward yourself.

You understand each platform at a native level - what works on TikTok is not what works on Pinterest. You adapt content without being told to. You manage the scheduling tool, maintain the calendar, and ensure no platform goes dark. Volume is the baseline - quality and brand accuracy are non-negotiable on top of it.

Scorecard - Outcomes

This is how we measure success. Not effort, not hours - outcomes with metrics and deadlines.

By Day 30
  • Full platform audit complete: reviewed every active social channel (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, X, Facebook, Pinterest). Documented current posting frequency, engagement rates, follower counts, top-performing content, and gaps.
  • Scheduling tool set up and running: whether Publer, Hootsuite, Metricool, or equivalent - the publishing pipeline is operational. Content calendar is built 2-3 weeks in advance across all channels.
  • Content audit delivered: reviewed all existing content assets (UGC, branded content, creator output, founder content). Identified what's working, what's not, and what's missing. Gap analysis presented to Director of eCommerce.
  • AI toolkit established: identified and set up AI tools for content creation workflows - caption writing, thumbnail generation, video editing, repurposing long-form to short-form, etc. Demonstrating speed gains within the first month.
  • Relationship with creative team established: integrated into creative workflow. Understands the briefing process, content pipeline, and creative strategy direction. Contributing ideas, not just receiving assignments.
By Day 60
  • Publishing at full velocity: all active channels publishing on consistent, high-frequency cadences. No platform goes dark. Volume targets met every week.
  • Creating original content: you are producing original content yourself - scripting, shooting, editing, or directing. Not just repurposing existing assets. At least 3-5 original pieces per week created by you or under your direct creative direction.
  • Platform-native adaptation live: each platform gets content optimized for its format - hook structure, caption length, hashtag strategy, posting time, trending formats. Not the same post copy-pasted everywhere.
  • UGC and creator content pipeline running: sourcing, repurposing, and publishing UGC, athlete content, and creator output. Working with the creative team to brief and collect content from external sources.
  • Community engagement rhythm established: comments and DMs managed daily across all platforms. Brand voice is consistent. High-value community members and organic advocates flagged to leadership.
By Day 90
  • Engagement rates above industry benchmarks: measurably above supplement category averages for engagement rate across at least 2 primary platforms.
  • Content performance log running: a living database tracking which angles, hooks, formats, and content types drive measurable outcomes (engagement, followers, clicks, saves). Data informing the calendar going forward.
  • Creative pipeline friction reduced: the creative team's output is being published faster and with less friction than before you joined. You are a force multiplier on their work, not a bottleneck.
  • AI workflows producing results: at least 2-3 AI-powered workflows are saving meaningful time - content repurposing, caption generation, thumbnail creation, trend monitoring, or similar.
  • Organic is driving measurable traffic: link clicks, profile visits, and DTC/Amazon referral traffic from organic social are tracked and growing week over week.
By Month 6
  • All channels growing: follower growth, reach, and engagement improving quarter over quarter across every active platform.
  • Organic social is a real channel: not a branding afterthought. Organic is driving trackable revenue through profile link clicks, UTM-tagged content, and attributed traffic. Reported weekly alongside paid performance.
  • Content machine is self-sustaining: calendar runs 3+ weeks ahead at all times. Publishing cadence doesn't break when you take a day off. The system works, not just the person.
  • Director of eCommerce trusts the pipeline: organic social runs without daily oversight from leadership. You own it completely.
  • Brand integrity maintained: zero compliance issues, zero bad health claims published, zero brand voice inconsistencies.
Ongoing KPIs

Once the system is built, these are the numbers you own and review weekly. This is your scoreboard.

  • Publishing volume: total posts per week across all platforms. Target: consistent high-frequency output with no gaps.
  • Engagement rate by platform: likes, comments, shares, saves relative to reach. Target: above supplement category benchmarks.
  • Follower growth rate: net new followers per week/month across each platform.
  • Content created per week: original pieces created by you or under your direct creative direction. Target: 3-5+ per week minimum.
  • Organic traffic to site: link clicks and UTM-attributed sessions from organic social. Tracked weekly in revenue dashboard.
  • Content calendar lead time: how far ahead the calendar is built. Target: 2-3 weeks minimum at all times.
  • Community response time: average time to respond to DMs and comments across platforms.
  • Top/bottom content report: weekly snapshot of top 3 and bottom 3 performing posts with analysis of why.

Explicit Boundaries

This role does not own:

  • Paid media strategy, ad creative for paid campaigns, or media buying - that belongs to the revenue function
  • Product vision, formulation philosophy, or brand worldview - those belong to the Founder
  • Creative strategy direction for paid channels - you own organic, you collaborate on paid
  • Influencer contracting or partnership negotiation

You own organic social end to end. Publishing, content creation, community, scheduling, performance reporting, and the creative output that feeds all of it. You bring platform-level insight and flag opportunities proactively.

Decision Rights

Owns: All organic social publishing, content creation and creative direction for organic, scheduling tool management, content calendar, community engagement, UGC sourcing and repurposing, AI content workflows, and organic social performance reporting.

Reports to: Director of eCommerce.

Collaborates with: Creative Strategist on content briefs and campaign planning, creative team on asset production, CX on community escalations, and Founder on brand voice direction.

This Role Is Not
  • A social media manager who posts occasionally and monitors mentions
  • A strategist who builds decks about what we should post someday
  • A coordinator who waits for content to land in their inbox before acting
  • A generalist marketer with social as one of five responsibilities
  • Someone who can't create content themselves and only manages others

This is a creator-operator who lives in the platforms, makes content, directs content, ships it relentlessly, and treats organic social as a serious commercial lever.

Ideal Candidate
  • 2-5 years managing organic social for a consumer brand - supplement, fitness, wellness, or health-adjacent strongly preferred
  • Proven content creator - you have a portfolio of content you've made yourself. Not just managed, made.
  • Experience with scheduling and publishing tools (Publer, Hootsuite, Metricool, Later, Buffer, or equivalent) - you've built workflows, not just used them
  • Platform-native across at least 3 major channels with a clear understanding of algorithmic and format differences
  • Strong writer - captions, hooks, and CTAs that sound like a smart human who respects the audience's intelligence
  • Proficient with AI tools for content creation - ChatGPT, Claude, Canva AI, CapCut, Opus Clip, or similar. You use these daily, not occasionally
  • Genuine working knowledge of health, supplementation, and fitness - you can speak intelligently about ingredients and protocols without being coached
  • High standard for factual accuracy - in this brand, a bad health claim is a brand integrity failure, not a typo
  • U.S.-based - this is a U.S.-only hire

Bonus: You've grown a brand's organic social from minimal presence to a real audience. You've built the content machine, not just inherited one.
