Head of Social Media

This is an operator role, not a strategy role. You will publish relentlessly, understand each platform at a native level, and serve as the connective tissue between our creative team and our audience.

Brand Manager & Head of Growth

Remote (LatAm / Americas TZ preferred)

$35K–$55K base | $45K–$70K total comp

Role Purpose

The Head of Social Media owns Formulation Factory's organic presence across every major platform. Your mandate is volume, consistency, and brand fidelity — shipping platform-optimized content at a pace that keeps us top-of-mind in a category where attention is everything.

You will work directly with the Creative Strategist and content writers to turn briefs into published content. You will not be handed a fully built machine — you will help build it. The expectation is that you are self-directed, systems-minded, and obsessed with the details of how content performs on each individual platform.

Core Accountabilities

1. Platform Ownership

Full ownership of publishing across Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, YouTube Shorts, Pinterest, and X. You manage the scheduling pipeline in Hootsuite or equivalent, maintain a content calendar 2–3 weeks in advance across all channels, and ensure no platform goes dark. Volume is the baseline — quality and accuracy are non-negotiable on top of it.

2. Content Collaboration

Work hand-in-hand with the Creative Strategist and content writers to brief, review, and publish value-add content — educational, authority-building, and community-driven. You are the final step before content goes live: formatting, captioning, native optimization, and scheduling. You are also responsible for sourcing and repurposing UGC, athlete content, and creator output.

3. Platform-Native Adaptation

What works on TikTok is not what works on Pinterest. What performs on Instagram Reels does not translate directly to YouTube Shorts. You understand these distinctions at a granular level — hook structure, caption length, hashtag behavior, posting cadence, trending formats — and you adapt content accordingly without being told to.

4. Community and Engagement

Manage comments and DMs across all platforms. Build genuine relationships with the audience, respond with the brand's voice, and flag high-value community members and organic advocates to the Brand Manager. You are the first to know when audience sentiment shifts and the first to report it.

5. Performance Reporting

Deliver a weekly social performance snapshot covering follower growth, reach, engagement rate, and top and bottom performing posts by platform. Maintain a living content performance log that captures which angles, hooks, and formats drive measurable outcomes. Use this data to inform the content calendar going forward.

6. Brand Integrity

Every piece of content that goes out carries the Formulation Factory name. We are a brand built on transparency, scientific accuracy, and earned trust. You must be fluent enough in health, supplementation, and fitness to catch errors before they publish — and rigorous enough to never let marketing language override factual truth.

Explicit Boundaries

This role does not own:

  • Paid media strategy, ad creative, or media buying
  • Brand positioning or product philosophy — those decisions belong to the Founder
  • Creative strategy direction — you execute and optimize briefs, you don't set the direction
  • Influencer contracting or partnership negotiation
  • Final sign-off on campaign strategy or channel expansion decisions

You are expected to bring platform-level insight and flag opportunities proactively. Execution excellence is your ownership, not direction-setting.

Decision Rights & Key Interfaces

Owns: All publishing decisions, scheduling, platform-level formatting, community responses, and performance reporting within content approved by the creative team.

Reports to: Brand Manager and Head of Growth for strategic alignment; Founder for brand voice questions.

Collaborates closely with: Creative Strategist on content briefs and campaign planning, Content Writers on caption and copy review, Head of Growth on coordination with paid media, and CX Lead on community escalations.

This Role Is Not
  • A social media manager who posts occasionally and monitors mentions
  • A strategist who builds decks about what we should post someday
  • A coordinator who waits for content to land in their inbox before acting
  • A generalist marketer with social as one of five responsibilities

This is a volume operator who lives in the platforms, ships content relentlessly, and treats organic social as a serious commercial lever — not a branding afterthought.

Year-One Success Profile
  • All active channels are publishing on consistent, high-frequency cadences with no gaps
  • A content calendar system is built and running 3+ weeks ahead at all times
  • Platform-specific performance benchmarks are established and improving quarter over quarter
  • The creative team's output is being published faster and with less friction than before this person joined
  • Community engagement rates are measurably above industry benchmarks for the supplement category
  • The Brand Manager and Head of Growth trust the social pipeline to run without daily oversight
Ideal Candidate
  • 2–4+ years managing organic social for a consumer brand — supplement, fitness, wellness, or health-adjacent strongly preferred
  • Genuine working knowledge of health, supplementation, biohacking, and fitness; you can speak intelligently about ingredients, protocols, and the science behind them without being coached
  • Proven volume operator who has managed multi-platform, high-frequency publishing pipelines and has the scheduling tool experience to prove it
  • Proficient in Hootsuite, Later, Buffer, or equivalent — you've built workflows, not just used them
  • Platform-native across at least three major channels with a clear understanding of the algorithmic and format differences between them
  • Strong writer — captions, hooks, and CTAs that sound like a smart human who respects the audience's intelligence
  • High standard for factual accuracy — in this brand, a bad health claim is a brand integrity failure, not a typo
